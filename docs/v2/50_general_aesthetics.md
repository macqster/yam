# YAM v2 — GENERAL AESTHETICS (DARK DECO)

---

## PURPOSE

Define the overarching aesthetic philosophy of YAM as a **Dark Deco system**.

This document establishes the **visual and perceptual foundation** guiding all rendering, UI, and composition decisions.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Dark Deco is:

> structure revealed through light within darkness

It is a synthesis of:

- geometric clarity (Art Deco)
- high-contrast lighting (Noir)
- mood-driven composition (Dark aesthetic)

The term itself originates from the blend of Art Deco architecture and noir atmosphere used in works like Batman: The Animated Series citeturn0search2

---

## 1. STRUCTURE FIRST

Visual priority:

- silhouette clarity
- strong directional lines
- readable geometry

Art Deco emphasizes geometric forms, symmetry, and clean lines over organic complexity citeturn0search13

---

## 2. DARKNESS AS MEDIUM

Darkness is not absence.

It is:

> the primary visual field

Rules:

- empty space remains dark
- do not fill space unnecessarily
- negative space carries meaning

---

## 3. LIGHT AS REVELATION

Light does not fill — it reveals.

```text
light reveals form
darkness conceals form
```

Rules:

- highlight edges, not interiors
- avoid uniform lighting
- use light sparingly

---

## 4. CONTRAST OVER GRADIENT

Avoid soft transitions.

Prefer:

- hard contrast
- binary separation (light vs dark)

---

## 5. SILHOUETTE PRIORITY

Rendering priority:

```text
1. silhouette readability
2. structural clarity
3. minimal detail
```

---

## 6. REDUCTION PRINCIPLE

Less detail → stronger perception

Rules:

- remove unnecessary detail
- collapse complexity into shape
- prefer bold over intricate

---

## 7. GEOMETRIC LANGUAGE

Borrow from Art Deco:

- vertical emphasis
- angular transitions
- symmetry where applicable

Avoid:

- chaotic curvature
- uncontrolled organic forms

---

## 8. CONTROLLED ACCENT

Color is sparse and intentional.

```text
90% neutral/dark
10% accent
```

Rules:

- accents highlight structure
- never saturate full scene

---

## 9. DEPTH THROUGH CONTRAST

Depth is created by:

- brightness differences
- density variation

Not by:

- blur
- gradients

---

## 10. MOOD AS PRIMARY OUTPUT

System should evoke:

- calm tension
- controlled darkness
- spatial depth

Before:

- realism
- visual completeness

---

## 11. CONSISTENCY OVER VARIETY

Rules:

- same structure → same visual behavior
- avoid random stylistic variation
- enforce repeatable patterns

---

## 12. SYSTEM-DRIVEN BEAUTY

Aesthetic quality must emerge from:

- rules
- constraints
- structure

Not from:

- manual styling
- arbitrary decoration

---

## FINAL PRINCIPLE

```text
Dark Deco is not about adding detail
It is about removing everything that weakens the form
```
